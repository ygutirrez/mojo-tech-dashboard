## üö® Red Flags & Areas of Concern

### 1. Marketing Spend Cut by 37%
- 2023: $265,552 (9.9% of revenue)
- 2025: $167,853 (5.5% of revenue)

**Why this matters:** If revenue has been "stagnant" as you mentioned, cutting marketing by $100K could be contributing to that. You may be starving growth.

**Question:** Was this intentional? Did ROI on marketing drop, or was this a cost-cutting decision?

### 2. Direct Labor Growing Faster Than Revenue
- Revenue: +13%
- Direct Labor: +32%

**Possible causes:**
- Wage inflation / paying more per tech
- Lower route density (more drive time)
- Less efficient scheduling
- Hiring ahead of growth that didn't materialize

**Action:** Review revenue per technician and jobs per day metrics

### 3. Auto/Fleet Costs Up 60%
- 2023: $146,076 (5.5% of revenue)
- 2025: $233,046 (7.7% of revenue)

Breakdown in 2025:
- Auto Insurance: $126,886 (up from $90K)
- Fuel: $45,451
- Maintenance: $32,381

**Action:** Your auto insurance jumped $37K. Shop this aggressively.

### 4. Royalty Fee % Doesn't Match
- 2023: $305,079 / $2,678,248 = **11.4%**
- 2025: $281,285 / $3,027,366 = **9.3%**

**Questions:**
- Why did royalty % drop?
- Was there a franchise agreement change?
- Or is some revenue not subject to royalty (misting equipment sales)?

### 5. Credit Card Fees Climbing
- 2023: $56,710 (2.1%)
- 2025: $70,910 (2.3%)

You're paying more despite rates generally being negotiable at your volume.
